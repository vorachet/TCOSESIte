Our SE Meetings and a visit at DTI 

Welcome to the latest report of Emerging INCOSE Thailand!. This month we have a fascinating message given by stakeholders and on the plan to reconnect with the near INCOSE chapters.  Thank you for your time and attention in reading this report. A lot of participants go into summarizing this report. A special thanks to all the participants in the last two meetings in 2017. Without you, we are unable to add new progress to the Emerging INCOSE Thailand.

It has been a great time for us in the initiative of the SE/MBSE community in Thailand, a group of academia, professionals, and vendors. The year started with our interest in sharing engineering knowledge and practices among different groups and centered around the MBSE and INCOSE's materials and activities. We held several virtual sessions and we are very agreeable to see that SE is not a role that can be largely taught in educational institutions in Thailand, so we set the specific resource for critical thinking and provide regular meetings for our community. INCOSE members and nearby chapters are significant for us. Some of our members attended the INCOSE conferences. After having a number of meetings with INCOSE members, we have built confidence to set up the Emerging INCOSE chapter to make the best support for Thai systems engineers and organizations who value SE roles.

Meetings

The ultimate goal of this activity is to be the authorized Thai local chapter of the International Council on Systems Engineering (INCOSE) and is intended to be the focal point for the dissemination of systems engineering knowledge and promotion of systems engineering activities within Thailand.

The first step to creating this association was driven by several local meetings where the potential core team of interested people was established to investigate the possibilities. 

The 1st Informational Meeting

Our first meeting was held on 30 September 2017 at the International College, KMITL in Bangkok, Thailand.

Visit Defence Technology Institute (DTI)

12 Dec 2017: Our key team Dr. Vithaya Suharitdamrong, Thanaphon Chearanai, and Vorachet Jaroensawas receive an invitation to visit DTI and meet SE division people. Vorachet Jaroensawas presented an overview of DoD from the MBSE perspective, several MBSE application in relation to the SE division at DTI, and how a local chapter support SE/MBSE applications in a specific context given by our members.  

 

Next 

This month we somehow become sensitized to something that we will call the restart of our local SE community development.  It all started in 2017 and this report gives us the evidence that reflects the need for an SE role in Thailand. Many people enjoy attending and volunteering in our two meetings and are also in the process of creating an authorized chapter in the future.

Here at the working group of the Emerging INCOSE Thailand, we are always eager to provide opportunities to our participants to share their wisdom and experience in the creation of a local INCOSE chapter.  if you have ideas you would like to advise us, please don't hesitate to contact us. 

During several meetings we have, the most popular topic we've discussed, quite frankly, the biggest challenge of being a Systems Engineer is that most people don’t understand what a Systems Engineer does, and hence the importance of such a role to facilitate a successful outcome in delivering a system solution. Building a local INCOSE chapter is a great challenge for us.
