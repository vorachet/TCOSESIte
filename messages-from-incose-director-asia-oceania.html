Message from INCOSE Director Asia-Oceania

17 July 2022

It is a great privilege to introduce the emerging Thailand chapter, on its journey to join the Asia Oceania Sector, one of three sectors of the International Counsel of System Engineering (INCOSE).

INCOSE is a not-for-profit membership organization that promotes international collaboration in systems engineering (SE) practice, education, and research with a vision for a better world through a systems approach. INCOSE’s mission is to ‘address complex societal and technical challenges by enabling, promoting, and advancing systems engineering and systems approaches’ (www.incose.org/about).

We believe the true challenges of today and tomorrow are systems challenges and that without the systems perspective, we cannot address these challenges effectively, efficiently, and free from unintended consequences. We find the interesting things are often found in the gaps – between disciplines, between technologies, between components.

INCOSE connects systems practitioners around the world – across organizational boundaries, domain boundaries, and national boundaries. Founded in 1990, INCOSE has over 65 chapters, 130 corporate advisory board members, and more than 20,000 members in 80 countries worldwide.

We create, advance, and broker information on systems approaches to help the broader community address complex challenges. Likewise we champion the systems perspective and the unique value of systems engineering and those who apply it.

Our Goals are:

  • To provide a focal point for dissemination of systems engineering knowledge.
  • To promote international collaboration in systems engineering practice, education, and research.
  • To assure the establishment of competitive, scale-able professional standards in the practice of systems engineering.
  • To improve the professional status of all persons engaged in the practice of systems engineering.
  • To encourage governmental and industrial support for research and educational programs that will improve the systems engineering process and its practice.

In addition to many local chapter and regional activities, INCOSE hosts two annual international events per year. The first is the International Workshop, which focuses on encouraging and promoting the activities of INCOSE’s technical Working Groups, the primary engine behind the products released by INCOSE Technical Operations. The second annual event is the International Symposium. This event is rich in content that promotes new research and state-of-the-art practice in SE. It also provides introductory content for those new to the discipline. These events attract more than 1,000 attendees from six continents each year.

The future creation of a chapter in Thailand is significant for the Asia Oceania Sector, where we witness a rapid increase in the need and practice for Systems Engineering and a systems approach, contributing to the local, national and global platforms.

As a local chapter and in line with INCOSE values, Emerging Thailand plays an essential role in serving and providing more opportunities for local members as per their local interest and at the same time bringing more diversity to the global community.

Among other things, a Chapter can:

  • Facilitate professional networking and information exchange,
  • Plan local activities like meetings, tutorials, workshops, mini-conferences and vendor fairs,
  • Contribute to working groups and projects
  • Disseminate announcements and news
  • Develop local initiatives such as Tutorials and handbooks, SE certificate programs, local Working Groups,
  • Organize activities with other professional organizations,
  • Interact with the greater INCOSE community.

Looking at the amount of activities already demonstrated by the Emerging Thailand Chapter, I am looking forward to the establishment of a full fledged Thailand Chapter for the benefit of all.

Serge LANDRY, CISM ESEP

INCOSE Director Asia-Oceania Sector
